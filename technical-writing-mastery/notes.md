## Session Notes

### Research on Technical Writing Best Practices (2024-2025)

**Key Findings:**

1. **Universal Principles**
   - Know your audience deeply
   - Establish a distinctive voice
   - Prioritize clarity above all

2. **User-Centered Design (UCD)**
   - Understanding user personas, goals, technical expertise levels
   - Organize content around user tasks
   - Use active voice, concise language, clear formatting
   - Break complex concepts into digestible chunks

3. **Accessibility & Inclusivity**
   - Create content accessible to everyone including people with disabilities
   - Use clear language, alt text for images
   - Ensure screen reader compatibility

4. **AI Integration (2025 trend)**
   - Use AI as a tool, not a replacement
   - Always verify AI-generated content accuracy
   - Add personal touch and expertise

5. **Collaboration**
   - Modern technical writing is increasingly collaborative
   - Use collaborative tools and platforms
   - Share experiences with other writers

6. **Good Documentation Practices (GDocP)**
   - Follow ALCOA-C principles: Attributable, Legible, Original, Contemporaneous, Accurate, Complete

### Key Insights from Research

**What Makes Top Technical Writers Great:**
- They have distinctive, recognizable voices
- They prioritize clarity and accessibility without dumbing down
- They provide complete, tested code examples
- They structure content for scannability
- They anticipate reader questions and confusion points
- They share personal experiences and real-world examples
- They update content to stay current

**Common Patterns in Excellent Technical Blogs:**
- Start with a compelling hook (problem, question, or story)
- Provide TL;DR for busy readers
- Use clear hierarchical structure
- Include multiple code examples with explanations
- Address common mistakes and edge cases
- Offer alternatives and acknowledge trade-offs
- End with clear next steps

**2025 Technical Writing Trends:**
- Increased focus on user-centered design
- AI as a tool but human expertise remains essential
- Greater emphasis on accessibility and inclusivity
- More collaborative writing processes
- Visual explanations gaining importance (diagrams, animations)
- Interactive code examples becoming standard
