# Week 1: Foundation & Baseline Assessment

**Theme**: Establishing Your Starting Point

**Week Goal**: Create baseline recordings, build awareness of current speaking patterns, establish daily habit.

---

## Day 1: Baseline Assessment

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Box breathing: Inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4 (repeat 5x)
- Lip flutters: 30 seconds
- Hum at different pitches: 30 seconds

**Main Exercise (15 min)**:
1. Choose topic: "My Professional Goals for the Next Year"
2. Set timer for 3 minutes
3. Record yourself speaking on this topic
4. **Do not listen yet** - just record
5. Record again on topic: "What I Want to Improve About My Communication"
6. Save both recordings with date labels

**Reflection (5 min)**:
- Download Ummo or Speeko app
- Write down 3 speaking situations you face regularly (meetings, presentations, networking, etc.)
- Commit to your daily practice time

**Success Metric**: Two baseline recordings completed and saved

---

## Day 2: Self-Analysis

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Deep diaphragmatic breathing: Hand on belly, feel expansion (10 breaths)
- Count 1-10 slowly, focusing on clear enunciation
- Tongue twister: "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" (5x, increasing speed)

**Main Exercise (15 min)**:
1. Listen to yesterday's recordings (audio only, don't watch)
2. Track these elements:
   - Filler words (um, uh, like, you know, so) - tally marks
   - Weak qualifiers (just, maybe, kind of, I think)
   - Speaking pace (does it feel rushed or too slow?)
   - Voice quality (clear/mumbled, strong/weak)
   - Pauses (natural or filled with "uh"?)
3. Create your "Awareness List" - write down top 3 patterns to improve

**Reflection (5 min)**:
Write: "The most surprising thing I noticed about my speaking was..."

**Success Metric**: Completed analysis with specific counts/observations

---

## Day 3: First Vocabulary Expansion

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Sharp exhales: Force air out quickly (30 seconds)
- Vocal slides: Slide from low to high pitch (1 minute)
- "Mmm" hums at various pitches (1 minute)
- Read this sentence slowly 3x: "Proper articulation requires deliberate attention"

**Main Exercise (20 min)**:
1. Read professional article in your field for 15 minutes
2. Identify and write down 5 new words you want to learn
3. For each word:
   - Look up definition
   - Write pronunciation guide
   - Create one original sentence using it
   - Say the word and sentence aloud 3 times

**Reflection (5 min)**:
- Add words to a vocabulary journal (digital or paper)
- Set phone reminder to use one word in conversation tomorrow

**Success Metric**: 5 new words documented with sentences

---

## Day 4: PREP Framework Introduction

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Box breathing (3 rounds)
- "Ha" exercise: Big breath in, force all air out on "ha" (5x)
- Lip trills while sliding pitch up and down

**Main Exercise (15 min)**:
1. Learn PREP framework:
   - **P**oint: State your main idea
   - **R**eason: Explain why it matters
   - **E**xample: Provide specific illustration
   - **P**oint: Restate conclusion

2. Practice with these prompts (2 minutes each):
   - "Why continuous learning matters in my career"
   - "The value of morning routines"
   - "Why communication skills impact success"

3. Record the third one
4. Listen back - did you follow PREP structure?

**Reflection (5 min)**:
Write out PREP structure for tomorrow's topic: "A skill I'm currently developing"

**Success Metric**: 3 PREP speeches completed, 1 recorded

---

## Day 5: Eliminating First Filler Word

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Diaphragmatic breathing (10 breaths)
- Count 1-20 with exaggerated mouth movements
- Tongue twister: "She sells seashells by the seashore" (5x)

**Main Exercise (20 min)**:
1. Review Day 2 analysis - identify your #1 filler word
2. Set up Ummo app to track that specific word
3. Speak for 3 minutes on "My typical workday" - record with app
4. Review filler word count
5. Re-record same topic, consciously replacing filler with pause
6. Compare counts

**Practice technique**: When you feel filler word coming:
- Take small breath
- Count "1" in your head
- Continue speaking

**Reflection (5 min)**:
Write: "I use [filler word] most when I'm [situation/feeling]..."

**Success Metric**: Measured reduction in filler word between first and second recording

---

## Day 6: Body Language Awareness

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Standing: Roll shoulders back 5x, forward 5x
- Neck rolls: Gentle circles both directions
- Power pose: Stand tall, hands on hips, 2 minutes (increases confidence)
- Deep breathing in power pose position

**Main Exercise (20 min)**:
1. Stand in front of mirror
2. Record yourself speaking on "A project I'm proud of" (3 minutes)
3. Watch recording (mute sound) and observe:
   - Posture (slouching, rigid, natural?)
   - Gestures (too many, too few, purposeful?)
   - Eye contact (looking at camera/mirror?)
   - Facial expressions (animated, flat, natural?)
   - Nervous habits (hair touching, swaying, fidgeting?)

4. Re-record with these adjustments:
   - Stand tall, spine straight
   - Shoulders back and down (not tense)
   - 2-3 deliberate gestures per minute
   - Smile when appropriate
   - Hold "eye contact" with camera 2-3 seconds at a time

**Reflection (5 min)**:
List 2 body language strengths and 2 areas to improve

**Success Metric**: Two recordings showing conscious improvement in body language

---

## Day 7: Week 1 Review & Assessment

**Warmup (5 min)**:
- Complete warmup sequence you liked best from this week
- Add one new element: vocal variety practice (say "Hello" in 5 different tones)

**Main Exercise (20 min)**:
1. Record "My Professional Goals for the Next Year" again (same as Day 1)
2. Compare to Day 1 baseline:
   - Filler word reduction?
   - Clarity improvement?
   - More confident delivery?
   - Better body language?

3. Watch/listen to both recordings
4. Document specific improvements

**Reflection (10 min)**:
Weekly review questions:
- What was hardest this week?
- What felt most natural?
- Which exercise had biggest impact?
- What will you focus on in Week 2?
- Rate your commitment (1-10): ___
- Rate your improvement (1-10): ___

**Success Metric**: Completed week 1 with measurable improvement in at least 2 areas

## Week 1 Notes

### Common Challenges

**"I feel awkward recording myself"**
- Normal! Everyone feels this way at first
- The discomfort decreases rapidly
- Recording is the fastest path to improvement
- You don't have to share recordings with anyone

**"I can't find 15-30 minutes daily"**
- Start with just 5 minutes (warmup + 1 exercise)
- Practice during commute, shower, or lunch break
- Consistency matters more than duration
- Block calendar time like any important meeting

**"I'm not seeing improvement yet"**
- Week 1 is about awareness, not perfection
- Progress compounds over time
- Re-listen to Day 1 vs Day 7 - improvements are there
- Keep going - Week 2 builds on this foundation
