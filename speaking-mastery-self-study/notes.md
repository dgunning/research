### Session 2: Core Speaking Fundamentals Research

**Key Self-Study Techniques Found:**
- Recording and self-review (listen to recordings to focus on words)
- Daily practice (5-10 minutes)
- Learning from TED talks and YouTube speakers
- PREP framework (Point, Reason, Example, Point)
- Managing anxiety through reframing as excitement
- Use bulleted lists instead of scripts

**Vocabulary Building Techniques:**
- Reading 20+ minutes daily (books, articles, audiobooks)
- Spaced repetition for new words
- Use 3-5 new words daily in conversation
- Context matters - use common words over overly fancy ones
- Professional vocabulary correlates with career success

**Speaking with Authority:**
- Lower pitch conveys authority
- Remove qualifiers: "just", "sorry", "I think", "maybe", "kind of"
- Use power pauses for emphasis
- Strong eye contact and open body language
- Avoid word fillers (uh, um, hmm, like, so)
- Speaking pace matters - too fast shows nervousness

**Empathetic Communication:**
- Active listening with attention to content and emotion
- Read nonverbal cues (facial expressions, body language)
- Social awareness through emotional intelligence
- Make people feel heard and valued
- 74% of managers say EQ is key skill for future (Capgemini study)

**Top Books Found:**
- "Talk Like TED" by Carmine Gallo
- "Confessions of a Public Speaker" by Scott Berkun
- "The Successful Speaker" by Grant Baldwin
- Dale Carnegie's public speaking books

**Top Courses Found:**
- Robin Roberts' MasterClass
- Harvard's Rhetoric course
- Rochester Institute of Technology Public Speaking Certification (edX)
- Skillshare public speaking classes

### Session 3: Practice Platforms & Voice Training

**Toastmasters Alternatives:**
- Dale Carnegie Training (8-week intensive, faster than Toastmasters)
- National Speakers Association (NSA) - professional networking
- Meetup groups for public speaking practice (free, flexible)
- Local debating clubs
- Improv classes (for impromptu speaking)
- Personalized coaching (one-on-one, customized)
- Public libraries and community centers (free workshops)
- Community college courses

**Online Learning Platforms:**
- Coursera, Udemy, Skillshare
- LinkedIn Learning
- edX courses
- Digital-first platforms (12-person max meetings)

**Voice Training & Vocal Exercises:**
- Diaphragmatic breathing (inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4)
- "Ha" exercise (force air out on "ha")
- Count 1-10 getting progressively louder
- Sharp exhales, vocal slides, lip flutters
- Good posture = increased airflow
- Ideal speaking pace: 140-170 words per minute
- Articulate slowly and carefully for clarity

**Public Speaking Apps:**

- Orai (interactive lessons, detailed analysis)
- Ummo (tracks filler words)
- VirtualSpeech (VR practice environments)

**Vocabulary Apps:**

### Session 4: Business Communication & Storytelling

**Executive Communication Skills:**
- Strong communication = career progression & six-figure salaries
- Communication is most crucial soft skill for career advancement
- Poor communication costs U.S. businesses $1.2 trillion/year
- Communicate like executives: use their language patterns
- Understand company goals and metrics

**Key Executive Skills:**
- Building trust and fostering collaboration
- Leadership is communication-based activity
- Communicating vision, ideas, feedback, results

**Storytelling Frameworks:**
1. Hero's Journey (Monomyth) - adventure, transformation, return
2. Three-Act Structure - Setup, Confrontation, Resolution
3. Story Mountain - climb obstacles to reach goal
4. Nested Loops - stories within stories
5. SCQA - Situation, Complication, Question, Answer
6. Sparklines - contrast present with hopeful future
7. Converging Ideas - diverse ideas align to common objective

**Storytelling Impact:**
- 63% remember stories vs 5% remember statistics (Heath research)
- Start with hook, use relatable characters, incorporate emotion
- Use contrast to make benefits undeniable
- Keep concise and focused

### Session 5: Daily Practice & Common Mistakes

**Daily Practice Routines:**
- Pick specific time each day for practice
- 2-3 minutes minimum, up to 1 hour
- Breathing exercises: inhale 4 counts, hold 4, exhale 6
- Mirror practice (see yourself speak)
- Record yourself (5 min talks, analyze filler words)
- Impromptu speaking (1 min timer, any topic)
- Practice anywhere: shower, office, outside
- No equipment needed except your body

**Top 10 Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
1. Inadequate preparation (causes "ums" and "uhs")
2. Not knowing your audience
3. Poor eye contact (2-3 seconds per person minimum)
4. Speaking too fast (nervousness indicator)
5. Low energy/monotone delivery (most disliked trait)
6. Data dumping (too much information)
7. Failing to engage audience
8. Using filler words (ah, um, like, you know)
9. Poor opening
10. Lack of call to action

**Body Language & Nonverbal Communication:**
- 55% of communication is nonverbal
- Stand tall but not rigid
- Eye contact makes audience feel valued
- Purposeful gestures emphasize points
- Walk across stage when transitioning topics
- Power of silence/effective pausing
- Upright posture = confidence
- Movement signifies changes in ideas

**Professional Networking & Elevator Pitch:**
- 30-60 seconds professional introduction
- Three C's: Clear, Concise, Compelling
- Components: Who you are, What you've done, What you want
- Use simple language, no jargon
- Builds connections and opens opportunities
- Practice with smile and handshake
