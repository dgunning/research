## Executive Summary

Thomson Reuters has positioned itself at the forefront of AI-powered legal technology through two flagship products: **Westlaw** (its decades-old legal research platform) and **CoCounsel** (its generative AI legal assistant acquired through the 2023 purchase of Casetext). In August 2025, Thomson Reuters unified these offerings under the banner of **CoCounsel Legal** and **Westlaw Advantage**, representing what the company calls the "final" version of Westlaw with continuous updates going forward.

**Key Highlights:**
- **CoCounsel** is built on multiple AI models (primarily OpenAI's GPT-4, with Claude for specialized workflows)
- **Westlaw** serves as the industry's most comprehensive legal research database with 40,000+ databases across 60+ countries
- **Over 20,000** law firms and corporate legal departments use CoCounsel, including the majority of Am Law 100 firms
- **Deep Research** capability launched in 2025 represents the legal industry's first professional-grade agentic AI
- Combined solution aims for **50-63% time savings** on legal research and document review tasks

## CoCounsel: AI Legal Assistant

### History and Acquisition

**Origins:**
- CoCounsel was originally developed by **Casetext**, a legal tech startup founded in 2013
- Initially focused on creating a community for attorneys to share knowledge and provide free access to legal texts
- Pivoted to embrace AI and machine learning for automated workflows

**Acquisition:**
- Thomson Reuters acquired Casetext for **$650 million in cash** in August 2023
- Strategic move aligned with Thomson Reuters' "build, partner, and buy" strategy for generative AI
- CoCounsel was already launched (March 1, 2023) before the acquisition

**AI Partnership:**
- Built on **OpenAI's GPT-4** large language model
- Casetext was granted early access to GPT-4 before public launch
- Official launch: March 1, 2023

### Product Versions

#### CoCounsel Core / CoCounsel Essentials

**Core Capabilities (8 Skills):**
1. **Prepare for a Deposition** - Creates outlines with topics and proposed questions
2. **Draft Correspondence** - Generates legal communications
3. **Search a Database** - Intelligent document search
4. **Review Documents** - AI-powered document analysis with citations
5. **Summarize a Document** - Condensed summaries of legal documents
6. **Extract Contract Data** - Pulls key information from contracts
7. **Contract Policy Compliance** - Identifies conflicts with stated policies and recommends redlines
8. **Timeline Creation** - Builds chronological timelines from case materials

**Pricing:** Starting at **$225/user/month**

**Target Use Cases:**
- Document-focused workflows
- Contract analysis and review
- Deposition preparation
- Basic legal research assistance

#### CoCounsel Legal (Launched August 2025)

The comprehensive flagship product that includes CoCounsel Essentials plus advanced capabilities.

**Enhanced Features:**

**1. Deep Research**
- Legal industry's first professional-grade agentic AI research capability
- Creates multi-step research plans autonomously
- Executes research iteratively using Westlaw and Practical Law content
- Delivers comprehensive reports with transparent reasoning
- Shows its work: traces logic and cites all sources
- Emulates best practices of expert legal researchers but works exponentially faster through parallel processing

**Key Differentiator:** Unlike traditional AI that summarizes search results, Deep Research actively plans, executes, and iterates on research strategies.

**2. Agentic Guided Workflows**

Multi-step task flows that apply agentic AI to complex legal work:
- **Document Drafting:** Complaints, discovery requests, discovery responses
- **Policy Creation:** Employee policies, privacy policies
- **Analysis:** Deposition transcript reviews, compliance risk assessments
- **Tax & Accounting:** Memo drafting, jurisdictional comparisons, compliance reviews

**How They Work:**
- Walk users through necessary steps with human oversight by design
- Provide links to relevant Practical Law resources
- Produce first drafts that users can review and refine
- Enable customization: lawyers can create and share workflows across practice groups
- Leverage structured reasoning embedded with legal content expertise

**3. Bulk Document Processing**
- Beta capability to review up to **10,000 documents** at once
- Announced at SYNERGY 2025 Legal Professionals conference (November 2025)

**Pricing:**
- **All Access Plan:** $400/month (invoiced annually)
- Volume discounts available
- Can be purchased as part of Westlaw Precision subscriptions

## Westlaw: Legal Research Platform

### History and Evolution

**Founding Era:**
- Founded by **John B. West** over 100 years ago
- Created unified system for reporting court decisions
- Established the first **National Reporter System**

**Key Number System Development:**
- Developed between **1897-1906**
- Comprehensive classification system of U.S. law
- Currently indexes over **110,000 legal topics and sub-topics**
- Attorney editors continue to hand-categorize cases to this day

**Digital Transformation:**
- **1975:** Westlaw digital platform launched - one of the first digital legal research platforms
- **1996:** West Publishing acquired by Thomson Corporation
- **2010:** WestlawNext launched
- **2018:** Westlaw Edge introduced with AI capabilities
- **2022:** Westlaw Precision launched
- **2025:** Westlaw Advantage - designated as the "final" major version

### Core Content and Coverage

**Scale:**
- Over **40,000 databases**
- Available in **60+ countries**
- **150+ years** of professional legal publications and expertise
- Content from **3,000+ subject matter experts**

**Content Types:**
- Case law (state and federal)
- Statutes and administrative codes
- Public records
- Law reviews
- Legal news and analysis
- Practical guidance and forms

### Key Features and Tools

#### 1. West Key Number System

**Overview:**
- Master classification system organizing all U.S. law
- **110,000+ topics and sub-topics**
- Every document indexed to relevant Key Numbers
- Enables researchers to find related cases across jurisdictions

**Ongoing Maintenance:**
- Attorney editors continue to categorize new cases
- Ensures consistency and quality of classification
- Forms the taxonomic backbone of AI-powered features

#### 2. KeyCite (Citation Verification)

**Core Functionality:**
- Determines whether cases or statutes are still "good law"
- Shows citing references and treatment history
- Critical tool for ensuring legal authority is current

**Advanced Features:**
- **KeyCite Overruling Risk:** Warns of cases invalidated implicitly
- **KeyCite Overruled in Part:** Red-striped flag for partial overruling
- **KeyCite Cited With:** Shows related cases cited together even if they don't cite each other
- **Citation Verification:** Can reveal unverifiable citations in others' work product

#### 3. Search Capabilities

**Natural Language Search:**
- Ask questions in plain English
- AI interprets intent and returns relevant results

**Boolean Search:**
- Traditional structured queries for precise control
- Supports complex search operators

**Retrieval Methods:**
- By citation
- By party name
- By KeyCite reference
- By specific case attributes (in Westlaw Precision)

#### 4. WestSearch Plus

**Technology:**
- Machine learning algorithms
- Natural language processing
- Citation network analysis
- West Key Number System integration

**Capabilities:**
- Returns relevant documents researchers are seeking
- Provides direct answers to thousands of types of legal questions
- Delivers results in seconds

#### 5. Litigation Analytics

**Insights Provided:**
- Judge behavior and ruling patterns
- Court statistics and trends
- Attorney track records
- Law firm performance data

**Use Cases:**
- Guide trial strategy decisions
- Select appropriate venues
- Assess likelihood of success on motions
- Prepare for specific judges

#### 6. Westlaw Today

**Content:**
- Curated legal news
- Written by attorneys and Reuters reporters
- Email alerts for relevant topics
- Keeps legal professionals informed of developments

### Version Comparison: Edge vs. Precision vs. Advantage

#### Westlaw Edge (2018)
- First major AI-powered version
- Introduced advanced search algorithms
- Enhanced KeyCite with machine learning
- Foundation for subsequent versions

#### Westlaw Precision (2022)

**Key Innovation:** Attribute-based searching with unprecedented granularity

**Precision Search Filters:**
- Legal issue and outcome
- Fact pattern and material facts
- Cause of action
- Motion type and outcome
- Party type
- Area of law

**Human-Powered Enhancement:**
- **250 attorney editors** hand-code case attributes
- More granular than traditional Key Number System
- "Old-school approach" to achieve precision

**Important Limitations:**
- Only available for about a dozen areas of law
- Cases from **2010 and later only**
- Does **not include unreported cases**
- Requires additional subscription cost

**Performance Claims:**
- Study with 101 attorneys showed:
  - Precision users found results in **20 minutes**
  - Edge users took **45 minutes** for same results
  - **Over 50% time savings**

**Additional Features:**
- **Browse Box:** Statement of issue, decision, legal issues, material facts
- **Outline Builder:** Drag-and-drop research organization, auto-formatted citations, export to Word
- **Keep List/Hide Details:** Save relevant cases, hide irrelevant ones
- **Graphical View of History:** Visual map of research path

#### Westlaw Advantage (2025)

**Strategic Positioning:**
- Designated as the **"final" major version**
- Future improvements delivered as **continuous updates**
- End of the version-number upgrade cycle

**Major New Features:**

**1. Deep Research with Agentic AI**
- Same Deep Research capability as CoCounsel Legal
- Creates research plans autonomously
- Executes iteratively
- Delivers comprehensive reports
- Transparent reasoning with full citations
- Grounded in Westlaw and Practical Law content

**2. Litigation Document Analyzer**
- Enhanced version of previous "Quick Check" tool
- Comprehensive analysis of briefs and motions
- **Identifies arguments in opposing motions**
- **Suggests counterarguments** with supporting legal authority
- **Language analysis feature:**
  - Examines entire briefs for potential mischaracterizations
  - Goes beyond quoted sections
  - Analyzes every legal assertion
  - Compares assertions against cited source material
  - Reveals unverifiable citations

**3. Enhanced Citation Verification**
- Beyond just verifying own citations
- Can analyze others' work product
- Identifies citations that can't be verified
- Critical for reviewing opposing counsel's briefs

**4. Full Integration with CoCounsel Legal**
- Seamless access to agentic workflows
- Unified interface across Westlaw, Practical Law, CoCounsel
- On-page analysis of content
- Move fluidly between research and AI assistance

## Technical Architecture

### Multi-Model AI Strategy

Thomson Reuters employs a **strategic multi-model approach**, selecting AI models based on specific workflow requirements:

#### Primary Model: OpenAI
- **GPT-4** powers core CoCounsel functionality
- Early access partnership (pre-public launch of GPT-4)
- **Custom LLM from OpenAI** being tested for specialized legal tasks
- **o1-mini model** tested for enhanced reasoning capabilities

#### Anthropic Claude
- Deployed via **Amazon Bedrock** for security
- Targeted at **high-sensitivity workflows**
- Primary use cases:
  - Tax and compliance work
  - Workflows requiring detailed customization
  - Tasks needing transparent reasoning chains

**Model Selection:**
- **Claude 3 Haiku:** Rapid processing tasks
- **Claude 3.5 Sonnet:** Deeper analyses requiring detailed insights
- Selected after extensive evaluation against automated and human expert benchmarks
- Anthropic's focus on safety aligned with Thomson Reuters values

#### Google Gemini
- Part of multi-model toolkit
- Specific workflow applications
- Diversifies AI capabilities

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Architecture

**Foundation:**
- Connects AI models to Thomson Reuters' vast knowledge base
- **150+ years** of professional legal publications
- Content from **3,000+ subject matter experts**
- **40,000+ databases** of legal information

**How It Works:**
1. User query received
2. System identifies relevant content from knowledge base
3. Content retrieved and provided as context to AI model
4. AI generates response grounded in authoritative sources
5. All responses include citations to source material

**Benefits:**
- Reduces hallucinations by grounding in factual content
- Provides transparency through citations
- Enables verification of AI outputs
- Maintains accuracy across legal specialties

### Infrastructure

**Cloud Platform:**
- Runs on **Amazon Web Services (AWS)**
- **Amazon Bedrock** for secure AI model deployment
- Enables rapid testing and deployment of new model versions
- Maintains strict security and compliance standards

**Performance Optimization:**
- **Prompt caching** for efficiency
- Parallel processing for Deep Research
- Strategic model selection based on task complexity
- Optimized for speed without sacrificing accuracy

### Agentic AI Architecture

**Multi-Agent System:**

The Deep Research capability uses multiple specialized agents:

1. **Research Agent:** Conducts searches and retrieves relevant cases and statutes
2. **Planning Agent:** Creates research strategy and determines next steps
3. **Discovery Agent:** Identifies gaps in research and new avenues to explore
4. **Orchestration Agent:** Coordinates workflow across agents

**Reasoning Process:**
- Agents work in parallel for speed
- Iterative refinement of research strategy
- Transparent reasoning chains show decision-making
- Human oversight built into design

**Emulating Expert Lawyers:**
- Follows best practices of experienced legal researchers
- Considers multiple research paths
- Identifies controlling authority
- Distinguishes cases and statutes
- Builds comprehensive arguments

## Competitive Landscape

### Primary Competitors

#### 1. LexisNexis

**Direct Competition:**
- Comparable legal research database to Westlaw
- Similar comprehensive coverage of case law and statutes
- Own citator service (Shepard's Citations vs. KeyCite)

**AI Response:**
- **Protégé Legal AI Assistant** launched August 2024
- Direct competitor to CoCounsel
- **Lexis+ AI** general AI research capability

**Strategic Alliance with Harvey AI:**
- Announced June 2025
- LexisNexis users can access:
  - Lexis generative AI capabilities
  - Shepard's Citations
  - U.S. primary law
  - All directly within Harvey interface
- RELX Group (LexisNexis parent) invested in Harvey's $300M Series B round (February 2025)

**Competitive Dynamics:**
- LexisNexis withdrew from major GenAI benchmarking study where Harvey and CoCounsel competed
- Alliance with Harvey positions LexisNexis to compete through partnership rather than direct development

#### 2. Harvey AI

**Company Profile:**
- **$5 billion valuation** (2025)
- **$75 million annual recurring revenue**
- Standalone legal AI platform
- Not tied to traditional legal research database

**Competitive Advantages:**
- **Accuracy leader:** Received highest overall score in major GenAI benchmarking study
- Thomson Reuters CoCounsel placed second
- **25-40% lower costs** for large-scale deployments vs. CoCounsel
- Positioned as more flexible and modern than legacy players

**Partnership Strategy:**
- Alliance with LexisNexis for content access
- Threatens Thomson Reuters' "content moat"
- Offers comparable research capabilities without requiring full Westlaw subscription

**Target Market:**
- Large law firms and corporate legal departments
- Organizations seeking best-in-class AI without legacy platform lock-in
- Price-sensitive customers at enterprise scale

#### 3. Other Competitors

**Callidus Legal AI:**
- Alternative AI legal assistant
- Positions itself as more affordable than CoCounsel
- Targets small to mid-size firms

**General AI Tools:**
- ChatGPT (OpenAI) - used directly by some lawyers despite risks
- Google Gemini - general-purpose AI
- Microsoft Copilot - productivity AI with some legal applications

**Competitive Disadvantage of General Tools:**
- Lack grounding in authoritative legal content
- Higher hallucination risk
- No built-in citation verification
- Not designed for legal professional standards

### Thomson Reuters Competitive Advantages

**1. Content Moat**
- 150+ years of proprietary content
- West Key Number System (unique taxonomy)
- Practical Law practice notes and templates
- Continuous attorney editorial oversight

**2. Integration**
- Unified platform across research, guidance, and AI
- Seamless workflow from research to drafting
- Single subscription for all capabilities

**3. Trust and Adoption**
- Established relationships with Am Law 100
- Majority market share in legal research
- Professional-grade accuracy standards
- Rigorous testing and quality control

**4. Multi-Model Flexibility**
- Not locked into single AI provider
- Can optimize for specific use cases
- Reduces risk of single-vendor dependency

### Competitive Challenges

**1. Harvey AI Threat**
- Demonstrated superior accuracy in benchmarking
- Lower cost for large deployments
- LexisNexis alliance provides content access
- Modern, flexible platform appeals to tech-forward firms

**2. Pricing Pressure**
- CoCounsel All Access at $400/month (annual billing)
- Harvey offers 25-40% savings at enterprise scale
- Smaller competitors target price-sensitive segments

**3. Innovation Pace**
- Rapid AI advancement requires continuous investment
