# Thomson Reuters Ventures: $150M Fund Focus Areas & Strategy

**Research Date:** November 21, 2025
**Fund Announced:** February 10, 2025

---

## Executive Summary

Thomson Reuters launched its **second Corporate Venture Capital Fund** ("Fund 2") valued at **$150 million** in February 2025, building on the success of its first $100 million fund launched in 2021. The fund focuses on early-stage technology companies across five key professional markets, with a strategic emphasis on AI and technologies that transform professional workflows. Fund 2 represents a continuation of Thomson Reuters' "Build, Partner, Buy" strategy to maintain innovation leadership.

## Investment Strategy & Criteria

### Investment Stage & Size

**Primary Focus:**
- **Series A investments** (approximately 80% of fund)
- **Flexibility:** Can invest in seed (earlier) or Series B (later)

**Check Size:**
- **Initial Investment:** $3 million - $7 million
- **Target Ownership:** 3% - 10%
- **Follow-on:** Available for portfolio companies

**Fund 2 Enhancement:**
The larger fund ($150M vs $100M) enables:
- Some later-stage rounds (Series B)
- Larger initial checks
- More follow-on capital for winners

### Investment Criteria

**1. Strategic Alignment**
- Must align with Thomson Reuters' core markets
- Technologies that augment existing offerings
- Can help Thomson Reuters' customers
- "We only invest in what we know"

**2. Competitive Consideration**
- Avoid backing direct competitors to Thomson Reuters divisions
- Challenge: Managing potential conflicts
- Focus on complementary vs. competitive technology

**3. Financial Discipline**
- Must meet traditional VC financial return standards
- Not just strategic value, but financial ROI expected
- Run like traditional VC, not just corporate development

**4. Technology Understanding**
- Deep vertical lens on technology
- Must understand the domain deeply
- Preference for technologies TR already uses or understands

**5. Product Clarity**
- Startups must be able to position product clearly
- "Being able to position your product in a way that people can buy it"
- Clear value proposition to professional customers

**6. Market Timing**
- Focus on technologies with "solid longevity"
- Currently bullish on AI: "AI is going to have solid longevity with new technology using AI for many years"

### Geographic Focus
- **Primary:** North America (United States, Canada)
- **Secondary:** Europe (UK investments like Detected)
- **Emerging:** Southeast Asia, India (based on Steffens' M12 experience)

### Sector Distribution (Based on Portfolio)
1. **Legal Tech** - Highest concentration (aligned with core business)
2. **Tax & Accounting** - Second highest (professional services)
3. **Fintech/RegTech** - Strong presence
4. **Risk & Fraud** - Compliance focus
5. **News & Media** - Smaller allocation

## Strategic Positioning & "Build, Partner, Buy"

### Thomson Reuters' Three-Pillar Strategy

**1. Build (Internal Development)**
- CoCounsel AI development
- Westlaw platform enhancements
- Internal AI research labs

**2. Partner (Strategic Alliances)**
- OpenAI partnership (GPT-4 for CoCounsel)
- Anthropic/Claude integration
- Cloud infrastructure partnerships (AWS Bedrock)

**3. Buy (Acquisitions & Investments)**
- **Large Acquisitions:** Casetext for $650M (2023)
- **Venture Investments:** Thomson Reuters Ventures
- **Seed-to-Acquisition:** Materia model (invest early, acquire if successful)

### Why Venture Capital Fits TR Strategy

**1. Early Technology Signals**
- See emerging technologies before competitors
- Understand market trends
- Test technologies with startups before building internally

**2. Optionality**
- Invest small (seed/Series A)
- Observe performance
- Acquire successful companies (Materia model)
- Partner with others
- Let unsuccessful investments exit naturally

**3. Lower Risk Than M&A**
- $3-7M investment vs $650M acquisition
- Test technology and team
- See market validation
- Option, not obligation, to acquire

**4. Customer Validation**
- Portfolio companies test with real customers
- TR can observe adoption and feedback
- Reduced risk of building wrong thing

**5. Innovation Speed**
- Startups move faster than large corporations
- Access to entrepreneurial talent
- Avoid "not invented here" syndrome

## Competitive Landscape for TRV

### Corporate VC Peers

**Direct Competitors (Similar Focus):**
- **LexisNexis Ventures** - Legal and regulatory tech
- **Bloomberg Beta** - Future of work, enterprise tech
- **Salesforce Ventures** - Enterprise software
- **Google Ventures (GV)** - Broad technology

**Differentiation:**
- **Deep Domain Expertise:** 150+ years in professional markets
- **Customer Access:** 20,000+ law firms, corporations
- **Integration Path:** Clear acquisition potential (Materia model)
- **Professional Focus:** Not generalist, specialized in 5 sectors

### Challenges

**1. Perception of Corporate VC**
- Sometimes seen as "slow money"
- Concerns about strategic conflicts
- Worries about IP and competitive information

**Mitigation:**
- Financial discipline (like traditional VC)
- 6-week diligence timeline
- Clear walls between VC and business units
- Proven exits (not just acquisitions by TR)

**2. Strategic Conflicts**
- What if portfolio company competes with TR?
- Information barriers required
- Difficult to navigate

**Steffens acknowledges:** "Difficult given the volume of promising opportunities"

**3. Limited Check Size vs. Growth Stage VCs**
- $3-7M checks competitive at Series A
- May not compete at Series B+ (though Fund 2 adds flexibility)
- Some startups prefer pure financial investors
