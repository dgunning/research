# Leadership Skills Assessment

**Assessment Area**: Mentorship and Cross-Functional Collaboration
**Target Role**: Principal AI Engineer at Thomson Reuters
**Date**: November 2025

## Purpose

This assessment evaluates your leadership capabilities for a Principal Engineer role. At this level, you're expected to lead through influence, mentor engineers, and collaborate across functions to deliver impact.

---

## Assessment Framework

**Scoring Guide:**
- **5 - Expert**: Recognized leader, shapes culture, develops other leaders
- **4 - Advanced**: Consistently leads initiatives, strong mentor, broad influence
- **3 - Proficient**: Growing leadership skills, mentors individuals, collaborates well
- **2 - Developing**: Learning to lead, some mentorship experience
- **1 - Awareness**: Limited leadership experience

## Interview Preparation

### Leadership Interview Topics

Prepare STAR stories for each:

- [ ] Leading complex project (scope, timeline, outcome)
- [ ] Influencing without authority
- [ ] Handling technical disagreement
- [ ] Mentoring engineer to promotion
- [ ] Driving architectural decision
- [ ] Cross-functional collaboration success
- [ ] Handling project failure
- [ ] Building consensus among stakeholders
- [ ] Pushing back on leadership
- [ ] Growing team culture
- [ ] Managing conflict
- [ ] Driving organizational change

### Behavioral Questions to Prepare

1. "Tell me about a time you led a project that failed."
2. "Describe how you've mentored someone to promotion."
3. "How do you handle disagreement with senior leadership?"
4. "Tell me about a time you influenced a decision you didn't own."
5. "Describe your approach to building team culture."
6. "How do you balance competing priorities from stakeholders?"
7. "Tell me about a difficult piece of feedback you gave."
8. "Describe how you've grown your technical influence."
9. "How do you know when to escalate vs. handle yourself?"
10. "Tell me about a time you changed someone's mind."

### STAR Format Template

**Situation**: Brief context (1-2 sentences)
**Task**: Your responsibility/challenge
**Action**: What YOU did (be specific)
**Result**: Measurable outcome + learning
